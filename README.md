# EDITA — Cligés
**Ed**itorial Modelling and Computational **T**extual **A**nalysis for the Study of Textual Transmission: the Case of **Cligés**.

## Description

EDITA explores the modelling of textual variation in the manuscript tradition of *Cligés* (Chrétien de Troyes — 1175 ca.), combining digital scholarly editing, structured textual data, and computational approaches to textual transmission.

The project investigates how textual variance can be represented, structured, and analysed as data, in order to support both philological interpretation and quantitative analysis of manuscript traditions. Particular attention is given to the interaction between editorial modelling practices and computational methods for studying texts characterized by multiple textual states and contamination.

By articulating editorial theory, TEI-based encoding, and computational analysis, the project contributes to ongoing research on the formal representation of textual traditions and on the role of structured data in computational philology.

## Research context

The manuscript tradition of *Cligés* provides a relevant case study for investigating the modelling of textual variation in medieval literature. The coexistence of multiple textual states and the complexity of relationships between witnesses make it possible to explore how editorial encoding can support both representation and analysis of textual transmission.

The project is situated at the intersection of:

- digital scholarly editing
- computational philology
- textual data modelling
- quantitative approaches to textual traditions

## Objectives

The project aims to:

- model textual variation in the manuscript tradition of *Cligés*
- encode variant readings using TEI-XML
- structure textual data for computational analysis
- explore relationships between witnesses through variant distributions
- investigate the role of textual variance in the configuration of manuscript traditions
- support reproducible workflows in computational philology
- produce interoperable scholarly data

More broadly, the project examines how editorial modelling choices influence the analytical potential of textual data.

## Methodological perspective

EDITA combines philological expertise and computational experimentation in order to:

- represent textual variation through structured encoding
- formalise editorial knowledge as reusable data
- analyse variant patterns across witnesses
- explore stemmatological approaches to textual similarity
- investigate computational models of textual transmission
- test data structures suitable for representing complex textual traditions

## Repository contents

The repository gathers materials related to the modelling and analysis of textual variation in *Cligés*.

### TEI encoding

TEI-XML files encoding textual witnesses and variant readings.

These materials document modelling decisions concerning:

- segmentation of textual units
- representation of variants
- structuring of the critical apparatus
- encoding of manuscript-specific features

### variation modelling

data structures designed to represent textual variation as structured and reusable data.

These may include:

- structured representations of variant types
- experimental encoding strategies
- intermediate modelling formats

### collation and alignment workflows

materials supporting the comparison of witnesses and extraction of textual variants:

- CollateX outputs
- alignment tables
- scripts for variant extraction
- exploratory notebooks

### computational analysis

materials related to quantitative approaches to textual variation:

- exploratory statistical analyses
- structured datasets for computational modelling

### data processing workflows

scripts and notebooks supporting the preparation and transformation of textual data:

- TEI transformations
- normalization procedures
- structured data extraction

## Research perspective

EDITA contributes to the development of modelling frameworks in which textual variation is treated as structured data that can be analysed computationally.

By articulating editorial modelling, textual scholarship, and data processing methods, the project explores how digital scholarly editing can support new approaches to the study of textual transmission.

The repository therefore reflects an exploratory research process combining editorial modelling, computational experimentation, and theoretical reflection on textual variance.

## Data availability

Some editorial materials and base textual data related to the manuscript tradition of *Cligés* cannot be fully distributed in this repository due to editorial constraints.

The repository therefore provides:

- structured extracts
- illustrative examples
- transformation scripts
- documentation

rather than the complete set of primary sources or full editorial files.

Access to additional materials may be granted for academic research purposes upon request.
